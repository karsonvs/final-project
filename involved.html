Design

The Helping Hand Project's Design Team is primarily tasked with the systematic design and development of prosthetic hands for children in need. This team is specifically oriented towards individuals possessing experience or interest in Computer-Aided Design (CAD) development. Regular club meetings serve as a platform for the team to engage in comprehensive discussions regarding design prototypes, systematically iterating and assembling each version of the prosthetic hand. Noteworthy in their approach is the careful consideration of unique features associated with each child's case, with an emphasis on optimizing comfort for the eventual wearer. The team's activities underscore a methodical and purposeful approach to their role within the Helping Hand Project, where technical expertise converges with a commitment to addressing individualized requirements.

Support

The Support Team within the framework of the Helping Hand Project assumes a pivotal role in outreach and communication with the families of the children being assisted by the club. Primarily tasked with facilitating interactions and conveying the specific needs and desires of each case, the team plays a crucial role in maintaining effective communication channels. Beyond their communication responsibilities, the Support Team is also entrusted with coordinating social events, fostering a sense of community and support. Tailored for students with a less technical background, this team accommodates individuals with a keen interest in the interpersonal aspects of aid provision. Their contributions emphasize the importance of fostering relationships and ensuring seamless communication in the collaborative efforts of the Helping Hand Project.
